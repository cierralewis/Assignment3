Why Computer Science?

Ever since I could remember, I have always wanted to be a animator. The thought of taking a mere drawing, bringing it to life, giving it a story that can impact people of all ages struck a cord in me. Animation requires two of the most evolving and innovative things in our society: art and technology. So it was only natural to assume that when it was time to pick a major I originally chose Art. I thought I can gain skills on creating storyboards, characters; a transition that will lead me to Art. I was wrong. The art department at Spelman only catered to the generic types of visual art; painting, sculpting, drawing. Thinking back to what animation entails, my sophomore year I switched from art to computer science; and I haven't regretted it since.
